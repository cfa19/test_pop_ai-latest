# Credits Overview

Credits are the currency used to run activities on POPskills. Each time a user runs an
activity, credits are consumed from their balance.

---

## How credits work

- Every activity has a **credit cost** displayed on its card.
- Credits are deducted when the activity processes your data (not when the activity is opened).
- Organizations share a **common credit pool** across all their users.
- Individual users have their own personal credit balance.

---

## Check your balance

Navigate to **Dashboard > Finance & Analytics > Credits**.

The credits page shows:

- **Available credits** — credits you can use right now
- **Total credits** — all credits ever purchased
- **Used credits** — credits already consumed by activities
- **Expired credits** — credits that expired before being used
- A list of your **10 most recent transactions** (purchases, consumptions, adjustments)

---

## Credit transactions

Each transaction record includes:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Purchase, consumption, or adjustment |
| **Amount** | Number of credits added or deducted |
| **Reason** | What triggered the transaction (e.g., "CV Analysis", "Credit pack purchase") |
| **Date** | When the transaction occurred |
| **Balance after** | Remaining balance after this transaction |

The oldest purchased credits are consumed first (FIFO).

---

## Credit expiration

All purchased credits expire **1 year** after the purchase date. Expired credits cannot be used and are lost.

The platform warns you when credits are about to expire. A warning banner appears on the Finance page when any of your credit packs will expire within the next **30 days**. The banner shows how many credits are expiring and when, so you can use them before they are lost.

---

## Who manages credits

| Role | Capabilities |
|---|---|
| **Individual User** | View balance, purchase credits |
| **Org Admin** | View organization balance, purchase credits, view all transactions |
| **Consultant** | No direct credit management (uses organization credits) |
| **Beneficiary** | No direct credit management (uses organization credits) |
